I built a wireless car using Arduino and a Bluetooth module for a robo race competition in college, and we won! We used a mobile phone as the transmitter or remote.


- How It Works:

1. Data Transmission: The journey starts on your smartphone, where you transmit movement commands through a custom app.
2. Bluetooth Reception: The car's Bluetooth module seamlessly receives these wireless signals.
3. Arduino Interpretation: The Arduino microcontroller, the car's brain, interprets the data, determining direction and speed.
4. Digital Signal Conversion: The Arduino converts commands into digital signals the motors can understand.
5. Motor Driver Activation: These signals flow to the motor driver, which amplifies them to activate the motors.
6. Precise Motion Execution: The motors faithfully rotate, propelling the car in the specified direction and speed.
